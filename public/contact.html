FAQ.

Quick answers to common questions about traffic safety equipment selection and maintenance.

What information should I provide when ordering?

Specify your application (road work, parking lot, warehouse), quantity needed, and any environmental factors like high-traffic zones or extreme weather exposure. For custom reflective solutions or bulk orders, mention your timeline and delivery location so we can recommend the most suitable traffic cones and delineators for your project.

Which items work best at night or low-light conditions?

Products with high-intensity reflective sheeting or retroreflective bands provide maximum visibility after dark. Look for equipment rated ASTM D4956 Type III or higher for nighttime road work. Reflective traffic cones with 360-degree bands and delineators with prismatic sheeting ensure drivers see warnings from all angles, even in poor lighting.

Are your solutions suitable for both indoor and outdoor use?

Yes. Most traffic cones and delineators are UV-stabilized and weather-resistant, making them ideal for outdoor construction sites, parking lots, and roadways. Indoor applications like warehouses, event venues, and training facilities also benefit from the same durable materials. Choose weighted bases for windy outdoor areas and lighter models for frequent indoor repositioning.

How do I choose between traffic cones and delineators?

Traffic cones work best for temporary marking, construction zones, and areas requiring frequent relocation. Delineators (posts or flexible markers) are better for semi-permanent lane separation, parking guidance, or curve marking. Consider cone height (18", 28", 36") based on visibility distance, and use delineators when you need a slimmer profile or ground-mounted stability.

How should I maintain reflective surfaces for long-term effectiveness?

Clean reflective bands regularly with mild soap and water to remove dirt, grime, and road film that reduce visibility. Avoid abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals that can scratch or degrade reflective sheeting. Inspect equipment monthly for cracks, fading, or peeling reflective tape, and replace damaged units immediately to maintain safety standards and compliance.
