How to choose confidently.

Select traffic safety equipment based on your specific environment and visibility requirements. Understanding key specifications helps you make informed decisions that enhance safety and meet regulatory standards.

Visibility & Reflectivity

Effective traffic safety equipment relies on retroreflective materials that bounce light back to its source, ensuring visibility in low-light conditions. Night visibility is critical for preventing accidents—look for equipment with high-intensity prismatic sheeting that maintains brightness even at sharp angles. Consider the traffic cone warning signs construction sites require for 24/7 operations.

Materials & Durability

Choose UV-rated materials that resist fading and cracking under continuous sun exposure. Weather resistance ensures your investment lasts through rain, snow, and temperature extremes. Impact tolerance is essential—flexible PVC bases and reinforced collars withstand vehicle contact without breaking. Look for ASTM-certified products that meet industry standards.

Placement & Maintenance

Proper spacing guidance varies by application: construction zones typically require cones every 10-20 feet, while parking areas may need wider intervals. Regular upkeep includes cleaning reflective surfaces monthly and inspecting for damage after weather events. Strategic placement maximizes visibility while maintaining traffic flow—position equipment at decision points where drivers need clear guidance.
